Majdal Shams is a Druze town in the southern foothills of Mount Hermon, north of the Golan Heights
Majdal Shams is the largest of the four remaining Druze-Syrian communities on the Israeli-occupied side of Mount Hermon and the Golan Heights,together with Ein Qiniyye, Mas'ade and Buq'ata.
The name Majdal Shams is of Aramaic origin, meaning: "tower of sun," possibly in reference to the town's elevation.
